## Setting

Los Mexicanos sits in the thick of Ayia Napa with the kind of room that announces itself before you've sat down: full Mexican fit-out, woven colour everywhere, sombreros where you'd expect them and a few you wouldn't, candles in jars that have clearly lived several lives. The vibe is loud and easy — no jackets, no airs, no need to keep your voice down. Service is genuinely warm: the staff lean in like they actually like the place, recommendations come fast and unprompted, and nobody flinches when you order a second pitcher. The soundtrack runs through Latin pop and the inevitable mariachi covers without trying to set a mood you didn't sign up for. You sit down ready for something fun, and the first thing the kitchen sends out — a frosted pitcher of margaritas — confirms the contract.

## The meal

A pitcher of frozen strawberry margaritas, sugar-rimmed glasses, served cold enough to fog the glass. The opener everything else has to compete with.

- **S 4.8 · A 3.0**
- **S** — Sweet, refreshing, and the perfect way to start. A touch oversweet by the bottom of the glass, but I forgave it twice.
- **A** — Refreshing and easy to drink, but the strawberry reads a little artificial — like the syrup did most of the work.

### Fresh Tortilla Chips with Guac and Queso

A starter board: house guacamole with tomatoes, cilantro, and garlic, alongside a chili con queso loaded with jalapeños and spices, and warm tortilla chips fresh enough to still steam at the edges.

- **S 4.5 · A 5.0**
- **S** — The guac is absolutely perfect — bright, garlicky, properly seasoned. The queso is excellent but tips into heavy by the third dip.
- **A** — Fresh, garlicky guac and a queso that's cheesy, salty, and creamy in exactly the right ratio. Dangerous before mains.

Flour tortilla rolls filled with beef, smothered in chili con queso and jalapeños, plated with Mexican rice, refried beans, and sweetcorn.

Baby back pork ribs cooked in Mexican spice and BBQ sauce, served with a small side salad, fries, and coleslaw.

- **S 4.0 · A 4.8**

A combination plate of chicken, beef, and prawn fajitas served on a hot skillet of slow-cooked caramelised onions, with four flour tortillas, guacamole, sour cream, and pico de gallo on the side.

- **S 5.0 · A 5.0**
- **S** — Pure perfection. This is what you come here for. The beef is deeply flavoured, the chicken is juicy, and the prawns are extraordinary.
- **A** — The shrimp is perfectly cooked — you can taste the coal from the grill and there's a quiet heat under the seasoning. The beef and chicken are seasoned exactly right and properly tender. Nothing is overcooked, nothing is underdressed.

## Verdict

Los Mexicanos is the kind of place where the highs are very high and the misses are forgivable because the room is so easy to like. The frozen margaritas and the supremos fajitas are the entire point — order them, share them, and let the rest of the menu be a distraction. Service is warm and unhurried, the décor commits, and the bill won't punish you for ordering a second round. Come for the fajitas, stay for the margaritas, and skip the quesadilla.
