## Setting

McDonald's is McDonald's. You don't come here for atmosphere, you come for consistency, speed, and the specific flavour of nostalgia that lives between a sesame seed bun and a paper sleeve of fries. The headline today is the new **Big Arch** — McDonald's swing at a heavier, louder burger than the Big Mac — and we built an order around it: a four-piece nuggets, a modified double McChicken, the Big Arch itself, and a regulation portion of fries to keep things honest. Two of us, four dishes, and one fairly direct question: does the Big Arch deserve the billing?

## The meal

### 4-piece Chicken McNuggets

Four nuggets, hot from the fryer, crispy enough at the edges to crackle when you bit them.

- **S 4.7 · A 5.0**
- **S** — Excellent as always. Crunchy, savoury, an absolute delight. The benchmark you measure other fast-food chicken against.
- **A** — Delicious as always. The nuggets at McDonald's rarely go wrong, and these were extra crispy, which is always a plus. A clean five.

### Double McChicken (modified) with double cheese

The McChicken, ordered off-script: two chicken patties instead of one, double cheese, no other changes. A small build that punches above its weight.

- **S 3.0 · A 5.0**
- **S** — Gives a Big Mac but Chicken vibe. A nice modification on paper, but in the hand it tips into heavy — the second patty is the one that breaks the bun-to-filling balance for me.
- **A** — The double McChicken with the cheddar and the mayo — not the vegan creamy sauce — is more up my alley. It's creamy, the build is simple enough that you can taste each element, and nothing fights the chicken. Five out of five.

### The Big Arch

Two beef patties, fried crispy onions, fresh diced onions, shredded lettuce, pickles, white cheddar, and the new Big Arch sauce, on a sesame seed bun. The Big Mac's bigger, brasher cousin.

- **S 4.5 · A 3.0**
- **S** — It's a Big Mac on steroids. There's a spicy zing running through the sauce and the bun-to-meat ratio is genuinely well-judged — you actually taste the beef rather than the bread. The sauce can get a bit overpowering by the back half of the burger, and the crispy onions feel like they're solving a problem the burger didn't have. Overall, top-five territory for me. 4.5.
- **A** — The sauce is more flavourful than the Big Mac's — tangy and spicy in the right ratios — and the crispy onions add a real crunch, but I could do without them. When the onions hit the sauce, you get a faint barbecue note that I'm not personally chasing in a McDonald's burger. The sauce gets overpowering by the end, and the pickles are the wrong call here — they spoil the rest of the build for me. 3.0.

### Fries

The constant. A medium portion, fresh enough that the fryer salt was still visible.

- **S 5.0 · A 4.0**
- **S** — Always a five. There's no point pretending otherwise.
- **A** — Crispy, which is how I want them, but under-salted on this run — I had to top them up at the table. Strong four.

## Verdict

The Big Arch is a confident swing. The bun-to-meat ratio is the best McDonald's has put on a tray in a while, the sauce is genuinely more interesting than the Big Mac's, and the crunch from the fried onions earns its place — at least until you start tasting barbecue. The misses are real, though: the sauce overcommits by the last few bites, and the pickles are the wrong note in this particular chord. It's a top-five fast-food burger for one of us and a polite three for the other, which is exactly the kind of split a genuinely new build should produce. Order it once, decide for yourself, and ask them to go light on the sauce if you like a quieter finish.
