## Setting

Night Orchard hides behind a painted door the color of plum skin. Inside, the room is dim in the way theaters are dim: your pupils adjust, and then the plates start to glow. The open kitchen is visible but not performative—more workshop than stage.

## The meal

The menu announces itself as a walk through an orchard at night, which is the sort of premise that normally makes me reach for my pen like a dagger. Here, it worked because the fruit never shouted. A pear course arrived as a thin carpaccio with a whisper of blue cheese and toasted seed—sweetness as structure, not decoration.

The savory midpoint was a mushroom broth poured tableside into a bowl of grains and herbs. It smelled like wet earth and woodsmoke in a way that felt earned, not scented. The final savory plate—a small piece of pork with apple and mustard—was textbook balance: fat, acid, heat, all accounted for.

## Verdict

Night Orchard is doing the harder thing: a themed tasting that stays serious. Book if you can. Bring someone who will let you steal a bite of theirs anyway.
