## Setting

The lift opens on the top floor of the Adams Beach Hotel and the room does the talking. Glass on three sides, the Mediterranean stretched out flat and gold, and a sunset that lands inside your wine glass before it lands on your plate. The dress code is formal and the room rewards it: linen tablecloths, low and steady lighting, and a calming background DJ that hovers just under the conversation rather than over it. Service is the kind that anticipates — water glasses topped, cutlery swapped, jackets taken — without ever feeling rehearsed. You sit down already convinced. The kitchen still has to make the case, and over six courses, it largely does.

A chicken croquette — cheesy, with a sauce on top giving it a gentle kick of heat. Comfort in a bite, dressed up for the room.

- **S 4.7 · A 4.8**
- **S** — A clean, confident handshake. I would have happily eaten three more.
- **A** — The bite that set the tone for the rest of the dinner.

### Smoked Burrata Salad

Burrata topped with nectarines, strawberries, cherry tomatoes, pistachios, and a pistachio pesto dressing — served inside a smoked glass bowl so the smoke clings to whatever's most porous on the plate.

- **S 2.0 · A 2.5**
- **S** — The nectarines absorbed too much smokiness and dragged the whole bowl with them. Concept got in the way of the ingredients.
- **A** — The burrata and pistachio pesto were genuinely refreshing — the bites without nectarine were extremely pleasant. It's the fruit-and-smoke combination that doesn't land.

### Scallops Fregola

<Polaroid
  src="/reviews/glasshouse-lounge-restaurant/scallops.JPEG"
  alt="Scallops Fregola at Glasshouse Lounge"
  tilt={2}
/>

Pan-seared scallops over fregola pasta, dressed with a lemon-turmeric and fish jus, finished with caviar pearled across the top.

- **S 4.9 · A 4.0**
- **S** — Textbook sear. The plate is restrained, the cook is exact, and the sauce doesn't try to do the scallops' job for them.
- **A** — A bit overly fishy for me, but the citrus intensity of the jus pulled it back. The fregola was a first — the texture was an exciting surprise.

### Lamb Rack

<Polaroid
  src="/reviews/glasshouse-lounge-restaurant/lamb_rack.JPEG"
  alt="Lamb Rack at Glasshouse Lounge"
  tilt={-2}
/>

Lamb rack with polenta, aubergine, artichoke, beetroot purée, and a merlot jus tying the plate together.

- **S 2.0 · A 3.8**
- **S** — The plate itself was interesting — a real blend of flavours and intensities — but the lamb leaned gamier than I wanted, and once you notice it, it's all you taste.
- **A** — The polenta was nicely creamy. The aubergine was overly salty because of the goat cheese on it. The beetroot purée was sweet and complemented the lamb well, and the merlot jus was the sauce that pulled everything together.

### Chef's Signature Steak

<Polaroid
  src="/reviews/glasshouse-lounge-restaurant/chefs_signature_steak.JPEG"

A USDA striploin, black truffle laid across the top, set over parsnip purée with espresso foam, king oyster mushrooms, aubergine, and brussels sprouts on the side.

- **S 5.0 · A 4.8**
- **S** — Perfect. Just perfect.
- **A** — Cooked exactly to medium rare. The parsnip purée and espresso foam complemented it beautifully and the garnish was tasty across the board — only the aubergine let the side down again, salty from the goat cheese.

### Flamed Alaska

A finale with a sense of theatre — the meringue torched tableside, blistered into peaks, with strawberry and passion fruit gelato underneath, lemon and biscuit layered through, and a ring of grapes, strawberries, and redcurrants around the plate.

- **S 4.5 · A 4.0**
- **S** — The temperature contrast is the whole point and they nailed it. Hot sugar, cold cream, no compromise on either.
- **A** — A nice, refreshing end to the night.

## Verdict

Glasshouse Lounge could have coasted on the view alone — most rooftops in this part of the island do. It doesn't. The kitchen has discipline, the service has rhythm, and the room has the kind of sunset that makes you forgive small sins. There aren't many small sins to forgive. Go for an anniversary, a proposal, or any night you want to feel slightly more dressed than you needed to be. Stay through dessert. Order the steak.
