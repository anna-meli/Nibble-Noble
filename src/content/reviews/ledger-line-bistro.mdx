## Setting

Ledger Line is polished the way a new briefcase is polished: immaculate lines, flattering light, and a soundtrack that never risks an opinion. The crowd is jackets-off-but-still-on-call, and the host stand operates with the efficiency of a trading floor. You will feel underdressed in sneakers, even if nobody says it out loud.

## The meal

The opening snacks are clever—too clever once or twice. A tiny tart with smoked fish and dill read as Nordic nod; it was delicious, but it sat oddly next to a aggressively spiced chicken skin that wanted to be street food. The kitchen can clearly cook: a piece of halibut arrived with skin like glass, and the sauce had depth without weight.

Where Ledger Line wobbles is cohesion. Each course impressed on its own, yet the arc of the meal felt like a portfolio review rather than a story. Dessert—a chocolate crémeux with coffee and hazelnut—was the evening’s most confident chapter: focused, adult, and not trying to explain itself.

## Verdict

Ledger Line is a safe splurge if someone else is paying and you value precision over personality. If you want a meal that remembers your name, keep walking.
